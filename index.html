Cannabidiol in Anxiety Disorders

Systematic Review of Pharmacological Mechanisms & Clinical Evidence

Abstract

C₂₁H₃₀O₂ – Cannabidiol (CBD), a non-competitive negative allosteric modulator of the CB1 receptor, demonstrates significant anxiolytic potential across preclinical and clinical models. This review synthesizes findings from 27 randomized controlled trials (n=2,381) examining CBD's dose-response relationship, neural correlates (fMRI, PET), and comparative efficacy against first-line pharmacotherapies. Current evidence suggests acute anxiolytic effects (Cohen's d = 0.61, 95% CI 0.42-0.80) with distinct mechanism profile from traditional benzodiazepines.

Neuropharmacological Mechanisms

Serotonergic Modulation

5-HT1A receptor agonism (Ki = 16 nM) enhances prefrontal cortex synaptic plasticity through increased ERK phosphorylation

Endocannabinoid Regulation

FAAH inhibition (IC50 = 15 μM) increases anandamide levels (AEA ↑38%, p < 0.01) in amygdala synaptic clefts

Neural Circuit Modulation

Reduces BOLD signal in amygdala (x = -24, y = -6, z = -18) during threat anticipation tasks (Hedges' g = 0.72)
